MANGAS 2023

SUBSCRIBE
My Hero Academia Kohei Horikoshi

This popular shonen manga series follows the story of Izuku Midoriya, a young boy with no superpowers in a world where almost everyone has them. After a chance encounter with his idol, the world's greatest hero All Might, he gains the power to become a hero himself and enrolls in a prestigious academy for heroes in training.

Jujutsu Kaisen Gege Akutami

This supernatural horror manga series follows the story of Yuji Itadori, a high school student who becomes a jujutsu sorcerer after swallowing a cursed object to save his friends. He joins a secret organization of jujutsu sorcerers who hunt down cursed spirits to protect humanity from their malevolent influence.

Oshi No Ko Aka Akasaka and Mengo Yokoyari.

What transpires behind the scenes of the glittering showbiz industry? How far would you go for the sake of your beloved idol? What would you do if you found out reincarnation was real? The star of the show is Aquamarine Hoshino and the stage is but a mere facade. Will he manage to reach the climax before the world of glamour swallows him whole?

Kaiju N°8 Naoya Matsumoto

This sci-fi action manga series follows the story of Kafka Hibino, a sanitation worker who dreams of becoming a monster hunter. When he is accidentally exposed to a substance that turns him into a kaiju (giant monster), he joins a special defense force to fight against the other kaiju threatening humanity.

Spy x Family Tatsuya Endo

This action-comedy manga series follows the story of a spy named Twilight who is tasked with infiltrating a prestigious school to get close to a target. He decides to adopt a telepathic girl named Anya as his daughter and recruits an assassin named Yor to pose as his wife in order to create the perfect cover.

Blue Lock Muneyuki Kaneshiro and Yusuke Nomura

This sports manga series follows the story of a group of talented high school soccer players who are selected to participate in a special training program to create the ultimate striker for the national team. The competition is fierce as they fight for the chance to be the top scorer and secure a spot on the team.

Atelier of Witch Hat Kamome Shirahama

The series follows a girl named Coco, who dreams of being a witch, but in her world only those born with magical abilities are able to become witches. However, after meeting a witch named Qifrey, it seems like there may be hope for Coco after all.

Black Clover Yuki Tabata

This shonen manga series follows the story of Asta, a young orphan who dreams of becoming the Wizard King, the most powerful mage in the kingdom. Despite being born without any magic, he joins a magic knight squad and with the help of his friends, he sets out to save the kingdom from various threats.

One Punch Man ONE and Yusuke Murata

This action-comedy manga series follows the story of Saitama, a young man who becomes a superhero for fun after training to the point where he can defeat any opponent with a single punch. He faces various villains and monsters, often with the help of his sidekick Genos, and struggles to find a worthy opponent who can give him a real challenge.

When it comes to manga, the terms "old gen" and "new gen" are often used to describe different eras of manga and the styles, themes, and trends that were popular during those periods. Old gen manga typically refers to manga series that were created before the 2000s, often characterized by distinct art styles and storytelling techniques. These manga series tended to focus on traditional genres such as action, romance, and comedy, and often had a more episodic structure. On the other hand, new gen manga generally refers to manga series that have been created in the past two decades or so. These manga often feature more diverse themes and styles, with a greater emphasis on character development, storytelling, and social commentary. Many new gen manga also incorporate elements of popular culture, such as video games, anime, and social media. New gen manga has also been influenced by changes in technology, with many manga series now being published digitally as well as in print. This has led to the rise of webtoons and other online manga platforms, which have helped to democratize the manga industry and give more opportunities to up-and-coming manga creators. Overall, both old gen and new gen manga have their own unique qualities and strengths, and both continue to be popular among manga fans around the world.
