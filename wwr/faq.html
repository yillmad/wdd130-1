What is water rafting and what do I need to bring for the trip?

Water rafting is a recreational activity where you navigate a river or rapids in a raft. To prepare for the trip, we recommend bringing sunscreen, a hat, sunglasses, a change of clothes, and water shoes with a good grip. We provide all necessary safety equipment, such as life jackets and helmets.

What is included in the trip?

We will provide all necessary camping equipment. This includes a sleeping bag, foam pad, waterproof duffle bag, small waterproof day bag, tent and a cot, eating utensils and camping chair.We provided water and lemonade during the day. Coffee, tea and hot chocolate are provided during the morning and evening hours. 3 meals per day, plus snacks

Do I need prior experience to go water rafting?

No, you do not need prior experience to go water rafting. Our professional guides will provide a safety briefing and instruction on how to properly paddle and navigate the rapids.

What is the minimum age requirement for water rafting?

The minimum age requirement for water rafting varies depending on the location and the difficulty level of the rapids. Typically, children aged 6 or older can participate in lower difficulty levels with parental supervision. We recommend checking with us beforehand to ensure that your child meets the minimum age requirement for the trip.

Are there any health restrictions for water rafting?

Yes, there are some health restrictions for water rafting. Participants with heart conditions, respiratory issues, or recent surgeries may not be able to participate in the activity. We recommend consulting with your physician beforehand if you have any concerns about your ability to participate.

How far in advance should I book my trip?

If you have specific dates you need, we recommend booking a year in advance. If you can be more flexible 6 months in advance is usually sufficient.
